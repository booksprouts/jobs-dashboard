Context Graph

A Context Graph is your enterprise’s meaning layer: a governed map of entities + relationships + policies + decision traces that makes AI reliable.

Why it matters

  • Without data, no context. Without context, AI fails.
  • RAG can retrieve text; a Context Graph adds structure, constraints, lineage.
  • It makes AI auditable: “why was this allowed?” not just “what happened?”.

Two layers

  1. Operational context: identity, ownership, relationships, temporal state.
  2. Decision context: decision traces, policy versions, exceptions, approvals.

Concrete example: renewal exception

Imagine an AI agent proposing a 20% renewal discount even though policy caps it at 10%. A system of record might store only the outcome: “20% approved.”

Without a Context Graph

  • No reliable grounding across systems (CRM, support tickets, incident history).
  • Hard to answer: who approved, under which policy version, and why.
  • Audits become expensive; scale becomes risky.

With a Context Graph

  • Agent retrieves facts + relationships (account tier, outage history, churn risk).
  • Evaluates policy v3.2, finds precedent, routes approval to Finance.
  • Writes back decision with trace + citations so it’s defensible and repeatable.

Sources (for context-graph framing): OpenText AI Data Platform, Context graphs explainer (Substack), Graphlit: context layer.

How this ties to practical enterprise AI

  • Outcomes > demos: a Context Graph is what lets you run AI like a system (SLOs, audit trails, controls).
  • Trust at scale: policies, entitlements, retention, and sovereignty become machine-enforceable constraints.
  • Operational reality: AI succeeds when it knows ownership, relationships, and the "why" behind exceptions.
